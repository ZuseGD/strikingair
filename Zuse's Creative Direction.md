# Creative Direction & Core Mechanics: Untitled Autobattler Project

## **Core Philosophy: Evolution Through Attrition**
> "We are the sum total of our experiences. Those experiences – be they positive or negative – make us the person we are, at any given point in our lives." — B.J. Neblett
>
> "A self that goes on changing is a self that goes on living." — Virginia Woolf

We are building an *Oregon Trail*-style survival loop paired with a deep, psychologically driven autobattler. The characters are not static classes; they are living ledgers of their own trauma and victories. We are stripping away traditional EXP bars and skill trees. Instead, characters grow, adapt, or break purely through the experiences and environmental inputs they survive.

---

## **1. Progression: Trauma, Time, and Growth**
Progression is qualitative, hidden, and deeply systemic. The player manages a cognitive and physical pipeline rather than a traditional spreadsheet of stats.

*   **Qualitative UI:** The exact math is hidden during a run to maintain tension. Instead of seeing "+5 Luck," the player receives qualitative feedback ("You feel luckier," "The sword feels heavier"). The player must read the narrative and behavioral data of their team.
*   **The Crucible of Recovery:** Growth comes from overcoming adversity. A catastrophic event (e.g., losing an arm) instantly applies a massive debuff. However, if the player manages to keep that character alive through subsequent events and camp rests, the injury calcifies into a powerful permanent trait (e.g., "Veteran Survival: Immune to Critical Hits").
*   **Expedition Pacing:** The journey involves camp jobs, night watches, and route selections. These non-combat moments provide the necessary downtime to process trauma, manage stress, and prepare the psychological conditions for the next encounter.

## **2. Combat: The Psychological Autobattler**
Combat is autonomous, meaning player agency lies entirely in preparation, team synergy, and macro-strategy. Characters operate on behavioral logic that dynamically updates based on their mental state.

*   **Mindset-Driven Targeting:** Auto-targeting behavior is dictated by mindsets and playstyles rather than strict class overrides. An Orc might enter a "Flow State" to optimally target the highest threat, or fall into "Tunnel Vision" under stress, locking onto a single enemy regardless of optimal tactics. 
*   **Conditional Ecosystems:** All abilities are passive or conditional. A healer's ability doesn't trigger on a flat cooldown; it triggers only when specific environmental or social parameters are met (e.g., a frontline warrior drops below a certain HP/stress threshold).
*   **Social Friction:** Party composition introduces social psychology into team-building. Diverse parties (Humans, Elves, Dark Elves, Orcs, Tieflings) can develop synergy traits ("Trauma Bond") or suffer from prejudice/racism traits based on who they interact with and what encounters they survive together.

## **3. The Base Loop: The Macabre Lineage**
The overarching plot involves an inquisition securing demonic artifacts. This creates a corrupting progression loop for the player's stronghold.

*   **The Wacky Escalation:** New recruits start as completely blank slates. As the base grows from a Hamlet to a Castle fueled by abyssal energy, the available recruits become progressively stranger, featuring severe disfigurements, bizarre starting traits, and potent buffs.
*   **Experience Reincarnation:** To combat the rogue-lite death spiral, the player can harvest the experiences of a wiped "A-Team." These harvested memories can be injected into fresh recruits, effectively recycling souls to keep the crusade moving forward.
*   **The Mentor System:** Characters rarely survive long enough to retire, but when they do, they become mentors. A retired veteran can train new recruits, offering a small percentage chance to pass down their hard-earned, highly specific survival skills.

## **4. The Climax: The Tactical Pause & Intervention**
To prevent the player from being entirely passive, the game features a high-stakes, analytical intervention mechanic. This tests the player's competitive mindset and ability to calculate risk under severe pressure.

*   **The Freeze:** When a character is about to take a lethal blow, the autobattle automatically pauses. The frantic combat audio drops out. The player is forced to deliberately assess the board state.
*   **Desperation Gambles:** The player is presented with intervention options dynamically generated by the party's current psychological and physical state. These are not guaranteed saves; they are massive risks.
    *   *The Cold Sacrifice:* Let the character die. Predictable trauma for the survivors.
    *   *The Interception:* Requires a highly synergized ally (e.g., someone with a "Protector" trait) to take the hit. If they lack the stats, *both* characters might die.
    *   *The Abyssal Push:* Force the dying character back to their feet using harvested demonic energy. They survive with 1 HP but instantly acquire a catastrophic psychological trait (e.g., "Paranoia," causing them to attack teammates in the next room).
    *   *The Panic Rally:* A pure gamble rolled against the party's hidden "Resolve" data. Success staggers the enemy; failure results in the character's death and a permanent, run-ending morale collapse for the survivors.

### **Visual & Tonal Direction**
*   **Art Style:** High-contrast, atmospheric 2D pixel art. Focus is heavily placed on readability. The player must instantly parse character silhouettes, triggering traits, and shifting formations.
*   **Visual Evolution:** Characters physically change to reflect their ledgers. Survivors will display disfigurements, new posture, corrupted auras, or traits a human wouldn't normally possess, visually communicating their journey at a glance.
